## 3. South West vs UK Division Separation
**Findings:**
- `data/pages.json` has fields: `slug`, `title`, `body_content`, `meta_description`, `image`. **No explicit `division` or `region` field**.
- The distinction is **inferred** in `build.py`:
  - `is_uk_page = not (content or '').strip()` – if `body_content` is empty, it's considered UK.
  - UK pages get a dynamic template; South West (Cornwall/Devon) pages use the full HTML from `body_content`.
- This inference relies on the assumption that **all UK pages have empty `body_content`** and **all SW pages have non‑empty `body_content`**. A single mistake (e.g., adding content to a UK page, or emptying a SW page) would misclassify.
- No explicit validation or guard against cross‑contamination exists in `build.py`.
- No cross‑contamination incidents found in changelog or known issues.
- The bulk‑edit snapshot (`uk-titles-updated-34866-pages`) suggests a title update was performed; we do not have the script to verify its filter logic. Given that no explicit division field exists, the script likely relied on the same `body_content` heuristic – a risky assumption.

**Status:** **CONFIRMED RISK** – division is a **convention**, not enforced by data structure or code. A data error could silently misclassify pages and generate wrong templates, titles, and schema.

## Summary of Risks Found (Ranked by Severity)

1. **No post‑build verification** – build succeeds even if `send.php`, `homepage`, or other critical files are missing (conditional copies).
2. **No post‑deploy live verification** – no automatic HTTP checks after deploy to confirm files are live and correct.
3. **Division separation is inferred** – no explicit field; could be broken silently by data changes.
4. **No validation of bulk edits** – the 34,866‑page UK title update might have accidentally touched SW pages if its filter logic was flawed.
5. **No load‑bearing safety checks** – all safeguards are currently manual reminders.
